0. Overview

How do I not fail at life?

This is a question that I ask myself constantly. I have read and will continue to read many books on this topic. I can take the question away from myself, and ask "what differentiates between successful people and failures?" I can also play with the premise of the question, by asking "What is success? Is it money or happiness... and how much for how long?" These are really interesting questions, but at the end of the day, success is a practice not a philosophy. So here I am, putting success into practice by developing software that helps foster success.

That is the "why" behind this web application. I want to be successful, and I think other people do too. From the few books I have read and anecdotes that I have taken to heart, the "how" of being successful involves prioritizing goals, and then skillfully executing actions in furtherance of those goals. The "what", I am selling is a database of what goals we should be prioritizing, and what actions to take in furtherance of those goals.

1. Getting Started

This is a simple platform for managing your goals

Start using the application by heading to the login page at "<root>/entry". The current version of the application has 3 types of users: a demo user, a registered user, and a paid user. You can start as a demo user, and head over to the goals page at "<root>/goals". If you want to see all of the functionality of the app, you can head over to the index page, located at "<root>/".

A demo user will only be able to use the application for the duration that the page is open. A registered user will have data stored in local storage on their own device, and have a few more menu options available to them. A paid user will have their data stored in the cloud, so they can access their data from any device, and have access to all menu options and premium content such as reviews of books and research that we have done in this space.

Once logged in, you can add goals to your home page. A goal is an area of your life that you want to improve on. According to the book, "Eat the Frog!" by Brian Tracy, most people have three areas of life that their goals concentrate around:

  • Career
  • Relationship
  • Fitness

I encourage you to think of these three areas first because that's where most people start. However, that is only a surface level introspection. After writing down goals in those three areas, consider how they help with:

  • Managing Stress
  • Experiencing Joy

Once you have your goals written out, you should select a few that have the biggest impact on your life. These should be your priority until you have completed them. Warren Buffet has a 5/25 rule for staying focused. Basically, only try to accomplish 5 things in the foreseeable future, and actively avoid the other 20.

Version 1.2

Acting intentionally toward accomplishing ONE goal at a time is the key to success. This means figuring out what success means in the context of a goal by defining a metric to measure how well you are doing, and then executing a plan to push that metric in the right direction. The easiest way to track your progress when performing a test on yourself is keeping a blog or journal about your plans and progress. Thus the /sprint route was added

Four actions can be applied to a sprint using forms:

  • open-sprint-v1.2
  • add-to-sprint-v1.2
  • support-sprint-v1.2
  • close-sprint-v1.2

Creating a sprint with the open-sprint-v1.2 form shows that you have an intention to test yourself over a period of time. Your progress can be blogged about and tracked with the add-to-sprint-v1.2 form. Next, a third-party can show support through the support-sprint-v1.2 form. Supporting a sprint means someone gives something toward the sprint, and a picture is taken as proof! The book "Happy Money" goes into detail about how investing in others has synergistic effects that help both the giver and the receiver. Finally, closing your sprint through the close-sprint-v1.2 form represents that you have taken some actions, however small, in furtherance of the goal, and you are ready to lock-in the results. The results are recorded and will be published publicly in future versions.

An application such as this starts to grow many heads in a trap known as "feature creep." I do not intend to fall into that trap... however. Although version 1.1 went live, it was missing many of the features that would prove whether it was successful or not. Most importantly, although I have laid the ground work for it, I am not tracking whether users are actually gaining value from the application. Thus, version 1.2 needs aws-s3 storage added, and a user page that tracks retention intensity of each user (total number of engagements/number of days since registration).

3. FAQ

Q: Trouble Logging in?
A: Try email: "demo@mail.com" and password: "123". If that doesn't work the server is down. Otherwise, you might have to reset your password. Just email me at punknight16@gmail.com

Q: Why are goals limited to 30 characters?
A: These are really goal names. Later functionality will include a deeper look into goal descriptions.

Q: Why use this site and not paper and pencil?
A: As of version 1.2 the public blog should encourage a certain amount of accountability that a personal paper journal cannot provide. The goal of this site is to create tools to make people more successful. By publicly displaying your goals and struggles, we can use that data as a community to help everyone.
